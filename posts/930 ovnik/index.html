2 Abstract

Are wide receiver yard totals highly affected by their target share? Using data found, I noticed that the amount of targets a wide receiver gets is highly dependent on the amount of targets they get throughout the season.

3 Background

Wide receivers are often judged based on their total receiving lines, but these stats have many underlying factors, mostly including target share. A player who gets more chances to catch the ball with often get more yards, regardless if they are efficient with their targets or not. By taking a look at how a players targets relates to receiving yards, we can help us understand if volume alone propels a wide receiver.

3.1 Hypothesis and prediction

The Question is: does A wide receivers target share affect their total yards on the season?

My hypothesis is that wide receivers with a higher target share will get more receiving yards than those with a lower target share.

5 Results

According to the scatterplot, we can see that a majroity target share is correlated with higher season receiving yard totals.

6 Discussion

These results support the hypothesis of target share being an important factor in a wide receivers total receiving yards in a season. I noticed that the coefficient between the two was positive,leading for additional yards after every target.

6.1 Strengths and Limitations

Some strengths about this study was that the hypothesis was indeed supported. A negative is that the data is not necessarily easy to read, and that there are also other factors that can lead to a WRs receiving statistics, including YAC and their QBs health/performance.

6.2 Conclusion

In conclusion, we now know that a wide receivers yards per season is highly dependent on their target share. While target share is very important, other factors can influence a WRs performance. However, according to the graph there is a major correlation between the two, and seems to be the highest factor.
